# World Research Brief: Ethics of Representation in Queer Digital Spaces
## Topic 17: The Politics of the Virtual Body

---

## 1. Topic Scope
This brief explores the ethical challenges of representing queer identity through digital avatars. It focuses on the tension between identity exploration and heteronormative design bias, the risk of commodification, and the authority to depict marginalized experiences in virtual worlds.

---

## 4. Key Concepts Glossary
- **Embodied Visibility**: The act of consciously presenting a non-hetero identity through a virtual body. *Matters to QV33R*: Connects Mishima's body-encoding to digital media.
- **Heteronormative Design Bias**: The structural assumption of binary gender in software architecture. *Matters to QV33R*: Identifies the "Control System" of the platform itself.
- **Gender Incoherence**: Digital presentations that refuse to fit male/female categories. *Matters to QV33R*: A model for "Referential Blur."
- **Ethical Design**: Embedding community values into the code of the virtual world. *Matters to QV33R*: The "Infrastructure" requirement for a living archive.
