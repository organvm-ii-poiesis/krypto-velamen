# World Research Brief: Real-World Activism and Digital Engagement
## Topic 21: Borderless Protest

---

## 1. Topic Scope
This brief explores the bridge between virtual engagement and real-world queer activism. It focuses on how digital platforms facilitate collective action, transcend geographical boundaries, and enable protest in regions where physical assembly is restricted.

---

## 2. Reality vs Artifice Decomposition

| Subtopic | World A (Reality/Material) | World B (Artifice/Mediated) |
|----------|---------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Mobilization** | Physical marches, sit-ins, and direct action. | Hashtag activism, digital fund-raising, and viral awareness. |
| **Geography** | State borders and physical distance. | Borderless communication and global solidarity networks. |
| **Visibility** | Public assembly and "street" presence. | The digital "stage": reaching millions through social terminals. |
| **Risk** | Physical arrest and somatic violence. | Doxxing, digital surveillance, and "shadow-banning." |
