# World Research Brief: Measuring Impact and Gathering Feedback
## Topic 25: The Feedback Loop

---

## 1. Topic Scope
This brief outlines the methodologies for assessing the impact of the QV33R project on its participants and the broader community. It focuses on collecting feedback ethically, measuring emotional resonance, and evaluating the project's educational and archival value within its "worlds."

---

## 2. Reality vs Artifice Decomposition

| Subtopic | World A (Reality/Material) | World B (Artifice/Mediated) |
|----------|---------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Feedback** | Interviews, focus groups, and physical surveys. | Digital metrics, user engagement data, and virtual polls. |
| **Resonance** | Somatic and psychological impact on the user. | The "Algorithm" of recognition: what gets shared and saved. |
| **Archive** | Real-world usage of the repository by researchers. | The "Interactive Dashboard" as a tool for tracking knowledge growth. |
| **Privacy** | Ethical handling of personal data and "Risk Flags." | The "Masked" feedback loop: collecting data without doxxing. |

## 4. Key Concepts Glossary
- **Qualitative Impact**: Measuring meaning and resonance rather than just numbers. *Matters to QV33R*: Validates the "Artistic" goal.
- **Participatory Design**: Involving users in the creation of the feedback loop itself. *Matters to QV33R*: The "Public Creation" principle in assessment.
- **Ethical Data Handling**: Ensuring participant safety and privacy (Topic 8). *Matters to QV33R*: The "Goverance" requirement.
- **Emotional Resonance**: The degree to which the text "lands" with its intended audience. *Matters to QV33R*: The ultimate metric of the "Signal."
